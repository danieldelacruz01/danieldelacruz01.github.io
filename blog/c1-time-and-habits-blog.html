Time & Habits reflection


After reading through the resources on time management and developing good habits, I found the blog post on counterintuitive ways to be productive really interesting because of how much it challenged my understanding of what it means to be 'productive'. I always considered productivity to mean being able to accomplish a lot of tasks in any given day, but the post describes it more as a state of mental well-being. In other words, the work we do is only productive if it is meaningful to us and makes us feel good at the end of the day. I also like that the author writes about how the things that we do aren't always productive, but that doesn't mean they're not important

Although in the past I haven't really consciously thought about what techniques I use to be productive, I can see some parallels between what I just read about time & habits and what has worked for me in the past when working on a project. For example, starting small is a technique that I have used subconsciously when working on big projects like university assignments. I find that the task doesn't seem as dauting when you're only looking at small pieces at a time. However, it can be easy to put too much energy into one small part if you have a good flow going. I believe this is where timeboxing comes into play.

I see myself working on integrating timeboxing techniques into my work habits, as this will allow me to focus and, more importantly, de-focus on tasks in a more evenly distributed way. Toggl, the time-tracking tool used at EDA, has an in-built pomodoro timer feature which I will try out to see if it works for me. I also anticipate that myself and the teams I work with will be using the concept of Minimum Viable Product a lot throughout our EDA journey.
